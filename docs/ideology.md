# SCI Ideology

## The Core Belief
Songs are not generated. They are *excavated* from identity.

SCI is built on the belief that the most powerful music comes from:
1. **Knowing who is speaking** — persona, not just voice
2. **Having something real to say** — message, not just topic
3. **Structural intentionality** — every section earns its place
4. **Cultural honesty** — language as identity marker

## The Pipeline Philosophy
Most AI writing tools ask: "What do you want to write?"
SCI asks: "Who are you, and what are you carrying?"

The questions are confrontational by design — they are drawn from the WH-Question
songwriting framework, which treats each question category (Who, What, When, Where,
Why, How) as a lens for uncovering a different layer of the songwriter's truth.

## Why Section-by-Section Generation?
Freeform AI song generation produces generic output because the model has no
constraints. SCI forces the model to:
- Write ONE section at a time
- Serve a specific structural goal
- Maintain the established persona and tone
- Build on what was already written (context passing)

This mirrors how professional songwriters work: verse by verse, with purpose.

## Cultural Dimension
SCI treats language as identity. The Sheng/Kiswahili/English blend is not a
gimmick — it reflects the lived reality of many Kenyan and East African artists
who move fluidly between registers. The `styleMapper.js` module encodes this
explicitly, allowing the AI to code-switch authentically rather than randomly.

## Extensibility as a Value
SCI is designed to grow. New archetypes, new languages, new structural templates
can be added without touching the core pipeline. The engine is modular by design.
